My Books

Database Design and Implementation using MySQL

This book is produced from collective experience and knowledge from UCSY database courses and teaching several batches on fundamental database. It focuses on the basic concepts of database system such as database introduction, DDL, DML, relational algebra. It also has comprehensive discussion on ER modeling techniques and followed by database normalization.

Data Structures and Algorithms using Java

Emphasized on basic data structures and algorithm concepts taught in UCSY, this book is a collection of theories, examples, and comprehensive notes for new learners. It balances simplification of dense theories with actual Java implementation.

Linear Algebra

As part of Computing Mathematics Series, Linear Algebra establishes basic understanding of the field to new learners. It is also the first math subject that combines the paper-based calculation with computer-based problem solving. MatLab is used in companion with this subject.

Differential Equations and Numerical Analysis

DENA book introduces the wide fields of solving differential equations and numerical methods as alternative ways to get the approximate result. Fundamental concepts such as Laplace Transforms, Forier Analysis, and Euler's Methods are discussed.

Probability and Statistics Concepts

PSC book builds a good foundation for statistics based on the existing understanding of learners on probability. It further leads to topics such as data analysis, hypothesis testing, regression, and correlation which are fundamentals of machine learning and other AI-related fields.

Analysis of Algorithms

Analysis of Algorithms is an advanced extension to Data Structures and Algorithms subject. It introduces more complex concepts such as computation of algorithm complexities, dynamic programming, state machines, and advanced sorting algorithms.

Problem-Solving Techniques for Calculus-1

As the name goes, the Calculus 1 is the ultimate starting point for all engineering students and learners. It sets a foundation for other engineering and computing mathematics. A full discussion of differential calculus is followed by a brief introduction of integral calculus.

Calculus 2: Theory Notes and Workbook

Being the second part of university-level calculus, Calculus 2 mainly focus on integral calculus and its principles. Examples are integration by parts, by partial fractions, etc. It also gives a light glace over solving differential equations which would lead to a new field of Differential Equations.

Calculus 2: Theory Notes and Workbook

Discrete Mathematics is one of the fundamentals of computer science in that it teaches the way "discre objects" are arranged. It discusses on topics of sets, probability, functions, counting, number theory, and graphs. These concepts are tremendously useful for further studies of computer science subjects such as linear algebra, machine learning, and algorithms.
